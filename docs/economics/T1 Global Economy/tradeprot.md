# Trade Protection

## What is Trade Protection?

Trade Protection (or protectionism) refers to government policies tha restrict international trade to help domestic industries compete against foreign imports.

These policies are designed to protect local businesses and jobs from overseas competition

## Reasons for Protection

- Protection of infant industries
- Protection of employment during a recession
- Protection against the dumping of imports below factor cost
- Reducing a balance of payments deficit

## Types of protection

Tariffs - taxes on imported goods to make them more expensive

Quotas - Limits on the number or value of goods that can be imported

Subsidies - Financial support to local producers to help them compete

Import bans or regulations - Legal restrictions that block or slow certain imports
